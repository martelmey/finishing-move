Lister's Mandate

"Not in this lifetime," She thought
as she wept onto her heartache's lap.
"The chemistry of the times -
in this air, has molded us all
who partake, who live today,
in any of our century's chapters."
The shaken woman lifted her head,
from the new seat her greif had taken.
Any way kept for her by life,
one and all went but staggering back home.
The same circumstance passed on, mirrored,
in the image of all time's districts,
preceding or advancing from the one
in her life, she was restricted to be.
Each of them's bordered
in design and garments,
spoken on a soundtrack,
and detailed each with new heights and plights.
Such are the decades, our bulk of work
assuming a face, and named in numerals,
defines its own sentience.
"If it's not me, someone else
has remembered and whose suit
has taken after me,
in an utterly different point in time -
that I may fathom but through fantasy,
in scrying the forward's black
and opaque wall; or by faith alone
in whomever's lips move to tell the tale,
in any past-time's case."
But from nothing's juncture with everything,
where-from the first word was whispered
by the orchestrated tears in time,
resounding apart by lightyears,
over the unamde and endless acres
here that found their space -
she was put in where every reprise
of hers would suffer.
Where the few and far between
of her sisters in makeup,
scattered like that across the land,
echoed their kinly woes beneath
the hush this day and age smothered them.
"This era's understanding of the Word,
and its pronunciation etched
into their ways of life,
is the ritual for my demise and binding."
Those were the words
she wrote at once upon getting home,
following the rain, blight and injury
she sustained by the way's prescription,
in an aftermath of that
and the harrowed hours preceding,
detailing the thousandth power
her heart took of one event's few
but growing recitals.
Each additional place afforded
to intensity for multiplication,
reflected each new fracture in a mosaic
sprawling her self's sheerest minimum.
A whole - once, I'd been -
had bent in recent years as saplings do,
to grow 'round frustration,
it's cracking, division's product -
that'll culminate but for
subtraction's force
from her heart's integrity.
Yet in that same sliver of time
she sat draped over her desk
in the kerosene gleam that lit her room,
whereon had settled
a solemn tone of dusk,
which struck the curtain's she'd drawn
on entry, to diffuse the waning herald
of the south-slipping sun
out across the windowpane's hug of walls -
might her instance have writhed alone
in space and time, but not at all
in transposition, where in each place
could undergo translation as do tongues,
and put her by result into
some standard mid-western bedroom,
by more than one hundred years elapsed,
craned over paper and clutching ink,
to lament in strongest silence
the precisely-same, pained notion -
by that younger decade's rendition.
"I can't feel it -
the harmony plucked with discord
from all angles of the shape taken
and immune to tracing,
by the hypocrisy of drawing lines
on the spawn of chaos, our consciousness -
though in absence of birth,
I shalt never die -
I'm lent indifferent from all others,
the vibration my colours cast
on finding resonance,
no matter the weight with distance,
it may stand to crush the founder -
on the outskirts of sense,
my solitude's dissolved
by the closeness but here that's made,
with my own today to that
of my spirit's even distant relative."
Words as hers were scratched
in code over a dense volume's series,
kept and abridging decades,
ending only with the chariot to her soul's
finaly cycle of breath and blood.
Enduring twice her lifetime,
put by her nervous family
behind the estate's walls,
stood as a temple to their name -
someone having trickled down
to some tertiary branch,
on the whole's fringe of extinction
on the next century's turn,
found and with them, enough resonance
to print them for my bookshelf's holding,
200 years later.

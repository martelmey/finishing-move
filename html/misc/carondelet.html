FM Inc. / Carondelet

Misc

A small town's where
I'd wind up resting
quite peacefully,
even if its name
was Carondelet.
Shut up, as I was
an orphan, in a
rusty man's trailer
drinking like-water,
at the wolves' mercy.
Going to school, as
I did, destitute from
any at all of what
covertly, I craved:
matrimony's spice.
Nestled as I was
behind the desks of
the class's most
infamous of morons.
Yet not even in
hindsight, I'd declare
they're both the tightest
example of this town.
Gas pumps - I'd tell ya,
are crucial to avoid
after dark, when fists
seem hotter for faces.
Daytime's safer by far
to frequent, when the
mid-teens losers appear
for the mart's daily
sale to them, of nachos.
But scum as such shake
the town, in a
crooked bruteness only
their fathers hold.
